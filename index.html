Instrument Shopping Behavior

Taobao is the largest e-commerce in China. People buy all sorts of things there, including instruments. However, we find that the shopping behavior of Western instruments and Chinese instruments is very different, and price, size, location are all the influence factors. Besides that, we further analyze the purposes and qualities customers look into when searching in taobao.

01. Define the Topic

To define the topic, we first analyzed the Chinese traditional instruments dataset provided in class, and did some research on music visualization. Then, we did a brainstorm on all the data we can find and possible relationships to create.


Through the process, three main topics have emerged:

  1. Instrument history & culture;
  2. Instrument classification method;
  3. Instrument useage in film or opera.

After group discussion, we decided to focus on instrument usage in film scores and their composers because there are a lot of literature about music in martial art films and film as one form of pop art related most to contemporary life compared to other two topics. The initial research question is: What’s the most used Chinese traditional instrument in films and why?


As most of reference we could found in this topic are very qualitative and it’s hard to find the pre-made dataset, we decided to build our own dataset (Pic2) and use an online graph tool to visualize the data (Pic3) . For further analyzing the data, we import the dataset into NEO4J (Pic4), a visual graph database. In the database, we can conduct queries to answer our research questions.


In short, there’re two main findings: 

  1. erhu is the most used instruments in film scores;
  2. Zhao Jiping is the composer who used most Chinese traditional instruments.

In order to further develop the research question, we did some interviews with potential audience. Through the interview, we found one common question interests both the audience and ourselves is: Why the composer choose certain instrument in certain film scene? However, when we try to compare certain instrument to the corresponding film scene type, the data is limited and the analysis process is complicated. It’s hard for us to find all the data needed and analyze these films manually. 


What’s more, we found that the former research question will be more relevant when it applies to normal people rather than only limited to prefessional users. So we came up with sales data in taobao, which is a much larger dataset and can reveal public’s shopping decisions. So, our final research question is: Why people choose certain instrument in taobao?

R.Q.

Why people choose certain instrument in taobao?

02. Hypothesis  

Before collecting data to answer our research question, we first generated some hypotheses about factors influencing instrument purchase decision. 


1) Price

The first obvious one is ‘price’. Since instrument can be as cheap as a cup of coffee or as expensive as a house, the most practical consideration of purchasing decision is can you afford it? So we assume that cheaper instruments are more affordable, so their sales counts will also be more.


2) Popularity 

Later on, when we came to one report(source) showing how popular piano is in China: 5.82 ownerships every 100 households, we started to question our first hypothesis. As piano is very expensive while well-known, we wondered whether popularity is another factor influences people’s buying decision.  

03. Collect Data

To understand instrument purchasing behaviors, we collect most of our data from Taobao. Through a data api service, we can search by keywords, just like we type keywords in Taobao app, and get the first page result in json format, which often has about 10 products. The result contains product name, id number, price and sales amount in a month. We need to set a parameter iteratively to get result in later pages, so we wrote a script to collect data of the top 50 products under each keywords. It turns out we have 2000 products in our dataset. 


Using the product id number, we can use another api to search for more detailed information about this product, such as storehouse location, market price, discount and so on. Due to the speed limitation of the api, searching for all the id can be time-consuming and also money-consuming (it cost 3 yuan for 100 search, so it will take 60 yuan to search for all), so we only search detail information for one five of the products to make a sample. During this process, we formatted the code into some function, so it may be useful for later projects if anyone wants to use the same api. And also because the service platform provide other api in a similar structure, the code can be modified quickly, which makes the later attempt to collect Bilibili data much easier.

04. Analysis & Visualization

1) Unbalanced market share

If we compare sales data of CI(Chinese Instrument) with WI(Western Instrument), we will find the market really unbalanced. WI has more than two times the sale of CI. Surprisingly, the sales of piano is even more than CI in total. And we can find that plucked instruments such as Guqin, Guzheng and pipa account for a large percentage of CI sales, while keyboard instrument(piano and Accordion) and bowed instruments(cello and violin) are the two biggest part in WI.

Pic5. Sale Count

2) Do cheaper instruments sell better?

Then we put price aside for a while and look into the monthly sale count. However we can see that the most profitable instrument may not be the best sellers. In CI, wind instruments such as Dizi account for more than half of the sales count. And in WI, while keyboard instrument still account for a relatively large portion, percussion instruments (xylophone and marimba) also sells well. We know that Dizi and xylophone are very cheap instrument, so we wondered whether there is tendency that the cheaper instruments sells better?

Pic6. Sales

3) Not Really. Both the cheapest and most expansive ones sell well.

We made this graph combined by bar chart and box plot. The bar height represent the monthly sales count, and the plot shows the range of the price. We can see the bars are gathered at the two end. Either the cheapest ones and the most expansive ones sell well, however nobody is interested in the ones at the middle.

The polarity is interesting and we want to know why.

Pic7. Sale Count & Price

4) What role is popularity playing?

Is it because of the popularity and well-knownness? From baidu index data, we found something interesting. The top 2 well-selled Chinese and Western instruments (CI: zhudi, pipa; WI: piano, trumpet) also show a polarizing trend. Combining the index data with sales data, the well-selled but expansive ones, such as piano and pipa, also have high search index. While the well-selled but less popular ones, such as Dizi and trumpet, are much cheaper in price.

So probably people buy the expansive ones because they are famous, and choose the cheap ones because they are more affordable? There must be different motivations behind the purchasing behavior.

Pic8. Baidu Search Index

5) Motivations behind the product names

To capture this motivation, we did a text analysis of the product names. The names in taobao are very interesting. Because the seller want the their products shown to as many people as possible, they tend to put every possible keywords in the name and make it really long. We use a keywords extract api to find the words that occurs most in the titles, and we can see that there are some patterns emerged. Based on semantic meaning, we can cluster the keywords into 5 groups: material, purpose, geolocation, style and user group.

Pic9. Text Analysis

After looking into purpose, we find that most categories has balanced emphasis on performing and graded examinations, except for percussion instruments and wind instruments. These two categories don’t have graded examinations, so in most cases people buy them for fun or as a music enlightenment tool for kids. And they are the cheapest and affordable ones, so people won’t have a hard time hesitating to buy or not to buy. 

Pic10. Instrument Purchasing Purpose

And for style preferences, we find that most WI have “hand-made” and “imported” tags to emphasize their high quality. Many CI are labeled “ancient” and “ethnic”. which suggest people associate them more with cultural meanings. There are also some tags like “mini” and “small groups”, which represent some specific needs of small groups. Interestingly, piano has a unique tag which is “intelligent”, that may because piano is the most modern instruments and there are many piano assisted by information technology to make it easier to learn. 

Pic11. Instrument Style Preference

05. Conclusion

So we tried to put median price, monthly sale count and baidu index together to look for some overall patterns. Each line represents one instrument and the color encodes their category. There are mainly two clusters. One has low price and low baidu index, but sells well, mostly percussion instruments and wind instruments. The other one has relatively medium price but the sale count is very small. There are mostly plucked instruments and bowed instruments in this cluster. From this two cluster we can say, price is a very influential factor behind the purchasing behavior. However we can also find some remarkable outliers. For example, violin has similar price and baidu index to chinese plucked instruments such as guzhing, but it sells way more better.

06. Learnings & Future Development

1) What we learned from the process

The exploring process of the datastory is challenging but worthwhile. To couclude, there’re mainly two things we learned from the trail and error.


• First is how to evaluate a topic.

As we changed our topic from films to sales in half-way, it wasted a lot of time. However, through this, we also realized the importance to evaluate the topic before going further. As a result, we summarized three key questions to ask when deciding the topic: 1) Can you get the data needed? 2) Can you analyze the data into the form wanted? 3) Can the analyzed result arouse audience’s interst?


• Second thing we learned is to view data from different perspectives.

At first, we generated many hypotheses about factors influencing people’s purchasing decision. However, no single factor shows a direct causation with sale counts through the initial data analysis. Stucked for a while, we decided to review the data mannually to see if we can find any insights. After that, we combined different influence factors together to better understand purchasing decisions. 

2) What we can do next

The insights we found through the data analysis are interesting but also limited. To draw further impact, we can deepen our research question into: “why people choose certain instruments?”. So if anyone wants to conintue the research, there’re two promising directions to contribute:

• Combine offline sales data

• Combine the music consumption behavior on the content platform (e.g., Bilibili, NetEase Music, etc.)
